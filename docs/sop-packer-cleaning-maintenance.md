# SOP: Packer Cleaning and Maintenance Cleaning

## Purpose

This SOP defines the standard process for cleaning, inspecting, and maintaining packer areas so equipment stays clean, safe, reliable, and ready for production. The goal is to prevent food safety risks, reduce downtime, catch issues early, and keep the packers in a condition that supports quality egg flow.

## Scope

This applies to all packer areas, transfer points, conveyors, guards, sensors, reject areas, case packing areas, surrounding floors, and maintenance-access points related to the packers.

## Responsibility

### Production Team
- Perform routine cleaning during and after production.
- Keep the packer area clear of trash, broken eggs, egg buildup, cardboard, tools, and unnecessary items.
- Report abnormal conditions immediately.
- Complete assigned daily cleaning checks.

### Maintenance Team
- Complete maintenance cleaning that requires guards, panels, or access points to be opened.
- Inspect moving parts, sensors, air lines, belts, chains, bearings, motors, and wear points during cleaning.
- Correct minor issues during the cleaning window when safe and practical.
- Enter work orders for anything that cannot be corrected immediately.

### Lead / Supervisor
- Verify cleaning is completed to standard.
- Confirm equipment is safe to restart.
- Escalate repeat issues, missed cleaning, or equipment concerns.

### Director / Manager
- Review trends from cleaning checks, downtime, work orders, and repeat issues.
- Assign corrective actions and improvement projects.
- Hold the team accountable to the standard.

## Safety Requirements

Before cleaning or maintenance work begins:

1. Follow all site Lockout/Tagout requirements when guards are removed, equipment is opened, or hands/tools enter a danger zone.
2. Never clean around moving chains, belts, rollers, pinch points, or powered equipment unless the task is approved as a safe running-cleaning task.
3. Wear required PPE, including gloves, eye protection, hearing protection, and any chemical PPE listed on the chemical label or SDS.
4. Keep water and chemicals away from electrical panels, exposed wiring, motors, sensors, HMI screens, and control cabinets unless approved for washdown.
5. Use approved chemicals only.
6. Do not bypass guards, switches, or safety devices.
7. Report damaged guards, missing hardware, exposed wires, unsafe floors, or sharp edges immediately.

## Required Supplies

- Approved cleaner / sanitizer
- Brushes
- Scrapers approved for the area
- Towels or wipes
- Trash bags
- Wet floor signs
- Flashlight
- Basic hand tools for maintenance access
- Grease or lubricant if required by PM task
- Work order device or log sheet

## Daily Production Cleaning Standard

Daily cleaning is focused on keeping the packer area clean, clear, and ready for operation.

### 1. Area Preparation

- Remove loose trash, cardboard, broken packaging, labels, and unnecessary items.
- Remove broken eggs and egg residue from visible surfaces.
- Clear floor hazards around the packer.
- Confirm tools and parts are not left on or near equipment.

### 2. Packer Surface Cleaning

Clean the following areas:

- Infeed area
- Egg transfer points
- Reject area
- Case packing area
- Operator touch points
- External machine surfaces
- Guard exteriors
- Floor around packer
- Trash and scrap collection points

### 3. Visual Inspection During Cleaning

While cleaning, look for:

- Egg buildup
- Leaks
- Loose guards
- Loose bolts or missing hardware
- Damaged belts or rollers
- Chain buildup
- Sensor blockage
- Air leaks
- Abnormal noise
- Product flow issues
- Cracked, broken, or worn parts

Any abnormal condition must be reported to the lead/supervisor and entered as a work order if it cannot be corrected immediately.

## Maintenance Cleaning Standard

Maintenance cleaning is deeper cleaning completed with proper safety control and access.

### 1. Lockout / Safe Access

- Shut down equipment as required.
- Apply Lockout/Tagout when required.
- Verify zero energy before opening guards or reaching into equipment.
- Remove only the guards or covers needed for the task.

### 2. Deep Cleaning Points

Clean and inspect:

- Under guards
- Chains and sprockets
- Belts and pulleys
- Rollers
- Bearings and housings
- Motors and gearboxes exterior only unless assigned PM requires more
- Sensor faces and brackets
- Air cylinders and air lines
- Reject mechanisms
- Transfer points
- Drop zones
- Product contact transition areas
- Hidden buildup points

### 3. Mechanical Inspection

During maintenance cleaning, check for:

- Loose chain or belt tension issues
- Worn sprockets
- Cracked or damaged guards
- Missing fasteners
- Bearing noise or heat
- Gearbox leaks
- Motor issues
- Damaged wiring or loose connections
- Air leaks
- Misaligned guides
- Sensor alignment issues
- Buildup causing drag or poor egg flow

### 4. Corrective Action

- Correct minor issues immediately if safe and practical.
- Create a work order for any issue requiring parts, downtime, electrical support, fabrication, or follow-up.
- Mark urgent issues as high priority if they can affect safety, quality, sanitation, or production uptime.

## Cleaning Frequency

| Task | Owner | Frequency |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Remove trash, broken eggs, and loose debris | Production | Daily / as needed |
| Wipe external surfaces and operator touch points | Production | Daily |
| Clean visible transfer points | Production | Daily |
| Inspect for abnormal conditions | Production + Maintenance | Daily |
| Deep clean under guards and access points | Maintenance | Weekly or scheduled downtime |
| Inspect chains, belts, rollers, sensors, air lines, and guards | Maintenance | Weekly or scheduled downtime |
| Review repeat packer issues and work orders | Lead / Manager | Weekly |

## Restart Standard

Before restarting equipment:

1. Confirm all tools, rags, parts, and cleaning supplies are removed.
2. Confirm all guards, covers, and safety devices are reinstalled.
3. Confirm the area is dry and safe to walk/work in.
4. Confirm sensors are clean and aligned.
5. Confirm no loose hardware or parts remain inside the equipment.
6. Remove Lockout/Tagout only according to site procedure.
7. Start equipment and observe the first run for abnormal noise, vibration, tracking, air leaks, or product flow issues.

## Pass / Fail Criteria

### Pass

- No visible egg buildup in required cleaning areas.
- No trash, tools, or loose materials left around the packer.
- Guards are installed and secure.
- Sensors are clean and clear.
- Floors are safe and free of major slip/trip hazards.
- No known unsafe condition exists.
- Work orders are entered for open issues.

### Fail

- Egg buildup remains in the packer or transfer points.
- Guards are missing, loose, or not reinstalled.
- Tools, rags, trash, or parts are left in or around the machine.
- Sensors are blocked or dirty.
- Air leaks, electrical concerns, or unsafe mechanical conditions are ignored.
- Floor hazards are not corrected.
- Equipment is restarted before the area is safe and verified.

## Documentation

The responsible person must document:

- Date and time cleaning was completed
- Area cleaned
- Person completing the cleaning
- Pass / fail status
- Issues found
- Corrective actions completed
- Work order numbers for follow-up items

## Escalation

Escalate immediately when any of the following are found:

- Safety concern
- Food safety or sanitation concern
- Electrical issue
- Missing or damaged guard
- Repeated product flow problem
- Packer downtime risk
- Part failure or suspected failure
- Issue that cannot wait until the next scheduled PM

## Standard Statement

The packer is not considered ready until it is clean, inspected, guarded, safe, and documented. Cleaning is not just housekeeping. Cleaning is inspection. Inspection prevents downtime. Downtime costs money.
